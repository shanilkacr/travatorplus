Look at a map of Sri Lanka and it seems tiny — surely you can do it all. Then you
drive Kandy to Ella and four hours vanish over a mountain pass. Distance is a
poor guide here; **time on the road** is what shapes a realistic itinerary.

## Numbers worth memorising

- **Colombo → Kandy:** ~115 km, about **3.5 hours**. No full expressway; the
  climb past Kadugannawa is slow.
- **Kandy → Ella:** ~140 km, about **4 hours** by road through the highlands.
- **Colombo → Galle:** ~2 hours on the E01 southern expressway — one of the few
  genuinely fast drives.
- **Ella → the south coast:** about **4–4.5 hours**, descending from hills to
  beaches.
- **CMB airport → Colombo:** ~45 minutes; **airport → Negombo:** ~10 minutes.

## The rules these imply

**Don't stack two long drives in one day.** A morning of Kandy→Ella plus an
afternoon push to the coast is a wasted, car-bound day.

**Put the first or last night near the airport.** Because CMB sits north of
Colombo and city traffic is heavy, many trips start in Negombo or drive straight
to the cultural triangle, saving Colombo (or Galle) for the end.

**Take the train where it beats the car for scenery, not speed.** The Kandy–Ella
line is slower than driving but unforgettable; book reserved seats weeks ahead.

## Why we bake this in

Every Travator itinerary is grounded in these real times. If a day doesn't fit,
the plan won't pretend it does — it'll split the drive, move a night, or suggest
the train. The point isn't to see everything; it's to arrive unhurried.
