If it's your first time and you have a week, you don't need to be clever. There's
a well-worn loop that works precisely because it respects the roads and the
seasons. Here's the shape, and the logic underneath it.

## The route

1. **Arrive → Sigiriya** (cultural triangle). Skip Colombo; drive north.
2. **Sigiriya.** Dawn climb of the rock, Dambulla caves, an elephant safari at
   Minneriya or Kaudulla.
3. **Sigiriya → Kandy.** Spice garden en route, evening puja at the Temple of the
   Tooth.
4. **Kandy → Ella.** The scenic hill-country train, tea estates, the Nine Arch
   Bridge.
5. **Ella.** Sunrise on Little Adam's Peak, a slow day in the hills.
6. **Ella → south coast.** Descend to Mirissa or Unawatuna for the beach.
7. **Beach → airport.** A final swim, then the drive back for your flight.

## Why it flows this way

**It only climbs once and descends once.** You go up into the hills in the
middle and come down to the coast at the end — no backtracking over the same
passes.

**It front-loads the early mornings.** Sigiriya and Kandy reward dawn starts;
doing them first, while you're still jet-lagged and waking early, is a gift.

**It ends somewhere you won't want to leave.** Finishing on the coast means your
last memory is a slow beach day, not a highway.

## Making it yours

Swap the south coast for the east if you're travelling **May–September**. Add two
days and you can fold in Nuwara Eliya's tea country or a Yala safari without
rushing. Start the whole thing in the chat — describe your dates and it'll place
the nights for you.
