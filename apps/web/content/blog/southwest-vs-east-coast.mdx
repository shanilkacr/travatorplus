The single most useful thing to understand about a Sri Lanka beach trip is that
the island runs on two monsoons. There is almost always a coast that's calm,
sunny, and swimmable — the trick is matching your dates to the right side.

## The short version

- **South-west & south coasts** (Bentota, Galle, Unawatuna, Mirissa, Weligama):
  best roughly **December to April**.
- **East coast** (Trincomalee, Nilaveli, Pasikudah, Arugam Bay): best roughly
  **May to September**.

If you're coming in January for beaches, go south. If you're coming in July, go
east. Trying to swim on the wrong coast in the wrong month usually means grey
skies and a rough, closed-out sea.

## Why it happens

The south-west monsoon (Yala) brings wind and rain to the west and south from
about May to September, while the east stays dry. The north-east monsoon
(Maha) flips it from about December to February. **April and September** are the
gentle shoulder months when both coasts can be lovely at once.

## What this means for a two-week trip

You don't have to choose. A common shape is to spend the hill-country and
cultural-triangle days in the middle of the trip — those are good year-round —
and anchor the beach days on whichever coast is in season for your dates.

> Planning around whales? Mirissa's blue-whale season (roughly November–April)
> lines up neatly with south-coast swimming, so you get both in one stretch.

When you plan with Travator, it already knows this — ask for "beaches in July"
and it'll steer you east, not south.
